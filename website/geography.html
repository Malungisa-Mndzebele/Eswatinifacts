Physical Geography

Eswatini descends from west to east across three parallel veld zones, each with its own elevation, climate, and agricultural character.

Highveld (West)

  • Elevation 1,200–1,800 m
  • Mountains and plateaus, cooler climate (15–25°C)
  • Forestry and livestock

Middleveld (Central)

  • Elevation 700–1,200 m
  • Rolling hills, moderate climate (18–28°C)
  • Maize, vegetables and fruit

Lowveld (East)

  • Elevation 150–300 m
  • Hotter, drier climate (20–35°C)
  • Sugar cane, citrus and cotton

Climate

  • Subtropical with wet and dry seasons
  • Wet season October–March
  • Annual rainfall 500–1,500 mm

Natural Resources

  • Minerals: coal, diamonds, gold, iron ore
  • Pine and eucalyptus plantations
  • Rivers including the Great Usutu

Environmental Pressures

  • Deforestation and soil erosion
  • Seasonal water scarcity
  • Climate change and variable rainfall

People & Strategic Location

People & Languages

  • ~97% Swazi, ~2% Zulu
  • Official languages: siSwati and English
  • ~75% of the population lives in rural areas

Regional Position

  • Bordered by South Africa and Mozambique
  • Member of SACU, SADC and COMESA
  • A gateway between South Africa and Mozambique

Transport

  • Well-developed roads linking to South Africa
  • King Mswati III International Airport (near Manzini)
  • Multiple border crossings
