History

From the founding of the Swazi nation to the modern kingdom

Nearly three centuries of continuity under the Dlamini dynasty, shaped by migration, a British protectorate, independence, and one of the world's last absolute monarchies.

The Story in Four Eras

Pre-Colonial (before 1881)

  • Swazi people migrated from the Great Lakes region
  • King Ngwane III established the nation in the 1750s
  • Dlamini dynasty and the Tinkhundla system take root

Colonial Period (1881–1968)

  • King Mbandzeni sought British protection in 1881
  • Direct British administration from 1903
  • 1907 land partition gave two-thirds of land to settlers

Independence (1968)

  • Independence achieved on September 6, 1968
  • Constitutional monarchy under King Sobhuza II
  • Prince Makhosini Dlamini as first Prime Minister

Modern Monarchy (1968–present)

  • Sobhuza II suspended the constitution in 1973
  • King Mswati III crowned at age 18 in 1986
  • 2005 constitution; renamed Eswatini in 2018

Rulers & Turning Points

King Sobhuza II (1921–1982)

  • One of the longest reigns in recorded history
  • Strengthened traditional governance
  • Suspended the constitution and banned parties in 1973

King Mswati III (1986–present)

  • Re-established an absolute monarchy
  • Oversaw the 2005 constitution
  • Reign marked by HIV/AIDS and economic challenges

Recent History

  • 2018: country renamed from Swaziland to Eswatini
  • 2021: pro-democracy protests and crackdown
  • 2023: assassination of lawyer Thulani Maseko
