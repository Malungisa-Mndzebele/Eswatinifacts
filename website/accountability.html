The Eswatini Leaks

"Eswatini Leaks" is a body of investigative-journalism material focused on transparency and accountability in the kingdom, connected to the International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ) and covered by the Times of Eswatini. We are documenting and analysing this material rather than reproducing the underlying leaks.

What It Examines

  • Government transparency and secrecy
  • Gaps in accountability mechanisms
  • Public access to official information

Who Is Involved

  • The ICIJ, an international investigative network
  • Times of Eswatini coverage
  • Domestic investigative journalism despite restrictions

Why It Matters

  • A test of media freedom in a restrictive environment
  • A benchmark against international transparency standards
  • A driver of international pressure for reform

Our approach follows standard research ethics: verify before publishing, protect sources, and keep the analysis balanced. Related context lives in Eswatini Today.

The 2024 National Budget

The annual budget speech, delivered by the Minister of Finance in March 2024, is a key primary source on government economic policy — and, alongside the Sibaye submissions, on how policy is communicated to the public.

Fiscal Policy

  • Government spending priorities
  • Sources of revenue, including SACU receipts
  • Allocation across sectors

Sectoral Priorities

  • Health and education funding
  • Infrastructure investment
  • Social welfare and development programmes

Reading It Critically

  • How the budget addresses unemployment and poverty
  • The government's diversification strategy
  • Transparency of the presentation itself
