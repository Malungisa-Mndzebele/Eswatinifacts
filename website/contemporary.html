Politics & Human Rights

Eswatini remains one of the world's last absolute monarchies. Political parties are banned from contesting elections, and recent years have seen rising tension between a pro-democracy movement and the state.

Recent Developments

  • 2021: major pro-democracy protests and a violent crackdown
  • 2023: assassination of human rights lawyer Thulani Maseko
  • Ongoing suppression of political dissent

Human Rights Concerns

  • Reports of extrajudicial killings and torture
  • Arbitrary arrests of activists and protesters
  • Restrictions on expression, assembly and the press

International Response

  • UN and regional criticism of rights violations
  • Diplomatic pressure for reform
  • Calls in some quarters for accountability

Economy & Society

Economic Challenges

  • Heavy dependence on South Africa and SACU revenue
  • Limited diversification and a persistent skills gap
  • Modest foreign direct investment

Social Pressures

  • An overstretched health system facing HIV and TB
  • High youth unemployment and limited safety nets
  • Gender inequality and rural service gaps

Environment & Technology

  • Climate change affecting water and agriculture
  • A rural–urban digital divide
  • Growing use of social media for information

Looking Ahead

  • Calls for constitutional reform and democratization
  • Potential in regional integration and diversification
  • Open questions about long-term political stability
